Los Santos is truly one of the most audacious and must see cities of the world. The people are beautiful and their attitudes…well, that’s for you to discover. The city has everything… whether you’re jet-setting, roughing it or in between, it’s ALL here. GETTING THERE

GETTING THERE

If you’re coming in by air, your plane will touch down at the fourth busiest airport in the world, the Los Santos International Airport. Affectionately known as LSX, it was originally built as an airfield for the Los Santos Air National Guard. In 1942 after WWII, it was converted for commercial flights. Since then, millions of people have travelled through the terminals of LSX.

LSX is now the fourth largest airport in the world. The airport is made up of five gates that are connected by a U-shaped two level terminal. Kerbside check-in is available.

At times you may need to put some distance between yourself and those that are in pursuit. If this is the case, we recommend that you make your way to LSX or take the first train out of Los Santos. Train stations are located within the city limits at Vinewood and in Little Mexico.

NEIGHBOURHOODS TO EXPLORE

NEIGHBOURHOODS TO EXPLORE

RICHMAN

Richman is one of the most exclusive neighbourhoods in all of Los Santos. The residents of Richman are from the more traditional old money sect. The houses are grandiose and so are their contents. Not that we’re alluding to anything, but sometimes Richman residents tend to leave their doors unlocked. Find a Boxville truck and cruise around the neighbourhoods until you see something you like.

MULHOLLAND

Mulholland is another exclusive neighbourhood in Los Santos. This neighbourhood is mostly composed of the newly affluent; Vinewood actors, rappers and record producers. This is considered the hippest neighbourhood and if you’re talentless enough to be invited to a party at Mulholland, celebrity sightings are guaranteed. The drive through Mulholland offers some spectacular views of Los Santos, but do exercise caution, the roads can be very windy and narrow.

DOWNTOWN LOS SANTOS

As your flight approaches LSX, one of the first things you’ll notice are the Babel towers of Downtown Los Santos. If you think this is a breathtaking sight from the air, wait til you’re on the ground looking up. Feeling a little adventurous? Have a parachute handy? Some of the urban adrenaline junkies have been known to base jump off the skyscrapers in Downtown Los Santos…but the question is…do you have the güevos?

Our suggestion is that you give her the Coq, The World of Coq that is, located in the posh district of Rodeo. THE WELL STACKED PIZZA COMPANY

BINCO

Binco is a place where you get what you pay for…the clothes are cheap and so is the quality. A discount store located in the Ganton district; you can expect to find a large selection of budget priced plaid shirts, khakis, and sneakers. A word of advice for those not familiar with the inner city life of San Andreas please be sure to pick the right colours, because the wrong ones could kill you.

DIDIER SACHS

Located in the posh shopping district of Rodeo, Didier Sachs is one of the most exclusive retailers in Los Santos. If you’re in the neighbourhood for a fine tailored suit, jewel encrusted watch, fine shoes and other opulent accessories, then this is the store for you. Be forewarned, a second mortgage on your home may be necessary to cover the cost of your new outfit from Didier Sachs.

PROLAPS

The authority in athletic apparel and the outfitter of all the professional sports teams in San Andreas. With a full line of athletic apparel, ranging from team jerseys, shorts, footwear, hats, and speciality items such as motorcross helmets, boxing headgear, and even hockey masks, you’ll find everything for the sports enthusiast at Prolaps. The prices are high but remember, it’s endorsed by San Andreas’ professional athletes. Check out the flagship store located just west of Downtown Los Santos.

VICTIM

Trendsters and brand whores alike frequent this retail destination like flies on…well you know the rest. Over priced and over styled, this store caters to the young and the rich who have no qualms dropping $1,000 on a pair of denim pants. TEN GREEN BOTTLES

A local bar in Ganton, if you’re new in town we suggest you bypass this establishment. Located in the middle of Grove Street Families’ territory, this is the type of bar where trouble WILL find you, whether you’re looking for it or not. However, if you feel compelled to go we suggest you first stop off at the Ammu-Nation in downtown because you never know...

THE PIG PEN

The Pig Pen is located in the Los Flores district and is the oldest gentlemen’s club in Los Santos. The clientele at this fine establishment is quite varied, ranging from your average business types to gang members of Los Santos Vagos. Under the watchful eyes of the well armed bouncers, confrontations are not frequent but this is Los Santos and anything can happen. Just don’t bump into anyone, make eye contact or be disrespectful and you should be okay. Be sure to tip the ladies, after all it is hard work, but don’t fall in love.

WHAT NOT TO MISS

WHAT NOT TO MISS

VINEWOOD

Your trip to Los Santos would not be complete without a visit to the motion picture capital of the world; Vinewood. We recommend a walk on Vinewood Blvd, where the only stars you’ll see are the ones encased in concrete, on the Walk of Fame. However, if you’d like to see a real star, just call Vinewood’s Chamber of Commerce to see when the next induction ceremony will be held. Besides the Walk of Fame there are other interesting landmarks to be seen…such as The Cathay Theatre and The Wee Regal.

RODEO

If shopping is on your mind, then Rodeo is the place to visit. Designer clothing stores line the streets where money talks and everybody walks. This small area is the most expensive shopping district in San Andreas. Keep your eye out for budding actors and seasoned professionals as the Interglobal Television Studios is located near by.

SANTA MARIA & VERONA BEACH

Santa Maria and Verona beach are the oceanic playground for the beautiful residents of Los Santos. Sun seekers and health nuts alike flock to these beaches to show off their tans and muscles. The stunning stretch of golden sand that lines the shore is complemented by the famous Santa Maria Pier offering spectacular sunsets and ocean views. Just be sure to grab a bite at the famous Brown Starfish Bar and Grill.

VERDANT BLUFFS

With over 2,000 acres of natural terrain, Verdant Bluffs is the largest municipal park and urban wilderness area in all of San Andreas. Situated on top of Verdant Bluffs is the historic Los Santos Observatory. Ideal for picnics and nature walks, Verdant Bluffs offers spectacular views of Los Santos.
